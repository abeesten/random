We launched the Youth Policy Lab in 2016 to help strengthen interventions and improve outcomes for Michigan youth. Like you, we want policies and programs designed to benefit young people in the state of Michigan to have strong, tangible outcomes that improve the lives of children in the state, especially those growing up in low-income communities.

Non-profits, agencies, policymakers and philanthropists aim to make decisions based on best practices, cutting-edge research and real-time feedback on program impact — we seek to help in this endeavor.

In this, our first quarterly newsletter, we are excited to share with you some of the highlights of our first year. We have embarked on seven new projects with partners doing important work in education, workforce development, juvenile justice and health care, and we have recruited an exceptionally knowledgeable and committed team of 25+ faculty affiliates, external advisors and staff — all introduced below.

This newsletter is one of many ways we plan to communicate with you as we grow this initiative to inform youth-focused policy in Michigan. Please visit our website, share this email with others and get in touch — we will be glad to hear from you.

Sincerely,

Brian Jacob and Robin Jacob
Co-Directors, Youth Policy Lab



OUR APPROACH

We develop hands-on partnerships with Michigan agencies and organizations. We help our partners address questions important to them, and use data to help them enhance their programs. Our work takes a variety of forms:

  1. Leading partners through a systematic process that links organizational goals directly to day-to-day activities, which can help organizations identify the implicit assumptions behind their program models, address roadblocks, and make better use of scarce resources
  2. Building data systems that can more readily be used to measure program effectiveness
  3. Providing descriptive analyses to identify areas of need
  4. Conducting rapid-cycle randomized control trials (RCTs) to test small changes to program models
  5. Conducting rigorous evaluations of program effectiveness

WHY THE LAB, WHY NOW
Like many other rust belt regions, Michigan is working hard to redefine itself and create opportunities within a changing economy, shifts in population, and increasingly polarized state and federal governments.

In addition, many organizations are committing resources, time and talent to help with Michigan’s revitalization and continued growth. The organizations, partners and civic leaders working in Michigan’s highest-need communities operate with an unparalleled commitment and resilience, and are managing many thoughtful efforts statewide to address key challenges in education, workforce development, health care and other issue areas important to the Youth Policy lab.

The University of Michigan is committed to helping improve well-being for citizens of the state. As part of this effort, the Youth Policy Lab is focusing on providing support to agencies and organizations throughout the state whose services directly impact the outcomes of Michigan’s young people.
